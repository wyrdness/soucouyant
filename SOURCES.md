# Sources & References: Soucouyant

## Books

### Academic & Anthropological

- Herskovits, M.J. & Herskovits, F.S. (1947). *Trinidad Village*. Alfred A. Knopf.
  - Foundational anthropological study documenting soucouyant folklore in Trinidad

- Herskovits, M.J. (1941). *The Myth of the Negro Past*. Harper & Brothers.
  - Groundbreaking work on African cultural retentions in the Americas

- Beckwith, M.W. (1929). *Black Roadways: A Study of Jamaican Folk Life*. University of North Carolina Press.
  - Comparative Caribbean folklore including vampire traditions

- Warner-Lewis, M. (2003). *Central Africa in the Caribbean: Transcending Time, Transforming Cultures*. University of the West Indies Press. ISBN: 978-9766401054
  - African cultural continuities in Caribbean including supernatural beliefs

- Paton, D. & Forde, M. (Eds.). (2012). *Obeah and Other Powers: The Politics of Caribbean Religion and Healing*. Duke University Press. ISBN: 978-0822351344
  - Context for Caribbean spiritual practices including soucouyant beliefs

- Trotman, D.V. (1976). "The Yoruba and Orisha Worship in Trinidad and British Guiana: 1838-1870." *African Studies Review*, 19(2), 1-17.
  - Yoruba influences on Caribbean folklore

- Day, C.W. (1852). *Five Years' Residence in the West Indies*. Colburn & Co.

- Thomas, J.J. (1869). *The Theory and Practice of Creole Grammar*. Port of Spain: Chronicle Publishing Office.

## Research Ethics & Cultural Sensitivity

When researching soucouyant traditions:

- **Respect living traditions** — This is not merely historical curiosity
- **Center Caribbean voices** — Prioritize Caribbean and diaspora scholars
- **Acknowledge colonial context** — Understand historical power dynamics
- **Avoid sensationalism** — Treat with academic seriousness
- **Protect informants** — Respect privacy of those sharing beliefs
- **Give back to communities** — Share research with source communities
- **Recognize ongoing significance** — Not extinct folklore but living tradition
